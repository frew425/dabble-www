Why has making a website never been fun?

  • 1991

    Before Content Management Systems (CMS)

    When the first website launched, it was straight and simple. No bells, but no headaches.

    Why this sucks: If you were one of the few who wanted a website, no one knew how to do this stuff and had to hire an "internet magician" to make one for you.

  • 1997

    Content Management Tools & Blogs Arive

    The arival of common CMSs and blogs started to make building websites easy... or at least easier. You could know manage multiple pages from one backend site builder.

    Why this sucks: Sometimes CMS tools can be just as complicated as hard coding. They can be bulky and even though they make minor updates easy, major updates can be a real pain.

  • 2006

    Super User-Freindly CMS Tools

    Soon after CMS tools, it was obvious the average mom & pop shop needed something even easier to build their company website. So extremely easy and cheap CMS tools like SquareSpace and Wix were launched.

    Why this sucks: Easy and simple just means little customization and forcing you to work inside a box.

  • 2016

    Hire Customer Developer

    This option has really always been available and a fairly popular one, just leave it to the professionals. They may or may not use a CMS, but this opens you up to unlimited customization.

    Why this sucks: Expensive and making changes and can be very time consuming when you have to contact developers every time you want a minor change.

  • 2030

    Robots Make Everything

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do you cost?
Once we've outlined the project scope, we will create an estimate for the initial setup with a standard rate of $25/hour. From there it's simple, we charge a $100/month maintenance fee that includes 2 hours of work. Any extra hours needed will be billed accordingly.
How long does a normal project usually take?
Completely depends on the size of the project. A small 1-3 page site might take about 20 hours or so spread over a week. Bigger sites will obviously take longer.
Do you only use WordPres?
Not at all, we are familiar with most CMS tools and if you have a preference, we can work with that. If you need a more robust site, we can even been complete Django & Python database sites.
